The word cloud visually represents the frequency of words in the inaugural speeches of US presidents from 1789 to 1826. The larger the word, the more frequently it appears in the speeches. The most frequently used word appears to be government. “Public”, “people”, “states”, “every”, “may”, “great”, “country” and “united” are some of the larger sized words, suggesting that the speeches largely focused on the people of the United States and how the nation is or can be great.
